# Transport Rules

FRED/private transfers cannot operate until service terms, pricing, booking, cancellation, payment, driver/vehicle, insurance, privacy, and dispatch rules are owner-reviewed.

## Positioning Rules

- Do not call the service a taxi business.
- Use premium wording: private transfer, private local driver, door-to-door, pre-booked, premium vehicle, flight-monitored airport collection, family-run Garden Route service.
- Avoid cheap-positioning wording: taxi, budget, cheap lift, shuttle bus, Uber alternative.
- Compete on trust, care, reliability, and premium service, not lowest price.

## Payment And Booking Rules

- Human approval is required before confirming a booking.
- New clients and all airport/long-distance bookings should require a 50% deposit.
- Balance rules must be set before launch.
- Waiting must never be free. Waiting is a product and must be time-limited and charged.
- After-hours, public holiday, extra waiting, extra stop, and cleaning fees must be defined before public booking.

## Launch Compliance Gates

Before paid passenger service:

- insurer must confirm paid private passenger transport cover in writing;
- Western Cape public operating licence requirements must be checked;
- PrDP requirements must be checked for each driver;
- vehicle roadworthy/licensing must be clean;
- customer terms must include liability, luggage, waiting, cancellation, refund, incident, and no-medical-care wording;
- tax records must track revenue, kilometres, time, fuel, costs, and profit from the first booking.
